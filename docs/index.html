ℹ️About this data▾

This map shows E. coli bacteria results for the Lower American River. E. coli indicates fecal pollution and the possible presence of disease-causing organisms.

Swim status is based on the most recent sample at each station, using EPA recreational water-quality criteria:

  • Good — ≤126 MPN/100 mL
  • Caution — 127–235
  • Warning — 236–410
  • Unsafe — >410 (STV)

Bacteria levels change quickly with rain, runoff, and flow. A single result is a snapshot, not a guarantee.

🦠 Algal blooms shown as cyanobacteria-cluster markers are harmful algal bloom (HAB) reports from the CA State Water Board FHAB program, for the Sacramento / American & Sacramento River area. Each splotch is colored by advisory level and fades with age — bold means recently observed, faint means an older report. Most are reported by the public; only some are lab-tested, so cyanotoxins may be present even when not confirmed.

⚠️ Drowning hazards shown as red diamonds (e.g. Clay Banks, the Tiscornia/Discovery Park confluence) are a curated safety reference — not from the monitoring data and approximate. Water can be bacteriologically "Good" and still be deadly to swim due to cold water and current.

🌊 River flow is the live discharge at USGS gage 11446500 (American River at Fair Oaks), in cubic feet per second. Water flows downstream from Folsom/Lake Natoma (east) to the Sacramento River (west). Higher flow means colder, faster, more dangerous water; releases from Nimbus Dam can change it quickly.
